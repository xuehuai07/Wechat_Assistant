## Full-view comparison evidence

Both use a persistent narrow left rail, a calm pale canvas, a large centered welcome prompt, and a broad rounded composer directly below it. The application deliberately replaces Gemini product branding and navigation with private-agent and WeChat controls, but retains the reference's hierarchy, whitespace, restrained blue accent, and minimal surface treatment.

## Findings

### Required fidelity surfaces

- Fonts and typography: system Chinese and macOS fallback stack keeps the display question light and spacious while navigation, metrics, and control labels retain stronger hierarchy. No clipping or awkward wrapping was observed at the tested viewports.
- Spacing and layout rhythm: desktop rail proportion, centered empty state, rounded composer, panel padding, dividers, and mobile panel spacing are consistent. The empty-state composer was specifically corrected during this run.
- Colors and visual tokens: pale neutral canvas, white surfaces, restrained blue active states, and amber/green WeChat status colors preserve the reference's quiet visual balance while communicating operational state.
- Image quality and asset fidelity: there are no reference raster assets to reproduce. Product icons use the existing Phosphor icon set, and the QR asset remains generated by the local QR library when needed; no placeholder imagery is used.
- Copy and content: private-agent, WeChat, privacy, and runtime labels are coherent for the application's purpose and intentionally do not copy Gemini product text.
